Do you still remember these?

/ RPM

RPM

Rotations Per Minute — how fast the motor spins. Higher number = faster wheel.

/ EM1 + EM2

Two encoders

Left wheel (EM1) and right wheel (EM2) can be controlled independently. Negative = backwards.

/ Stop

Stop the motor

Always end your program with stop encoder motor, or the robot keeps rolling.

Two kinds of light patterns

2 min

Next class you'll program the mBot2's onboard LEDs to do two classic patterns. Watch them animate below — same 5 LEDs, totally different vibes.

Flow Light BUILD-UP

LEDs light up one by one and stay on — until all five are lit, then it resets and starts again. Like water filling up.

Running Light CHASE

One LED lights up at a time, then the next, then the next — like a single dot running across. Same as a marquee sign.

→ Don't worry about how to code these yet. Just notice the difference — it'll click in class.

Three quick warm-ups

10 min
No robot needed — these are quick brain warm-ups so we can move fast in class. You can do them on your phone or laptop. Tick each one as you go (it saves automatically).
1

Open mBlock and find the LED blocks

Open mBlock (the app you installed last time). Make sure mBot2 / CyberPi is selected as your device. Scroll through the block categories on the left and find the purple LED blocks.

Specifically, see if you can spot these three:

LED all ▾ displays ⬤
LED 1 ▾ displays ⬤
turn off LED all ▾

💡 Tip: they're under the purple category, usually labelled "Lights" or "LED".
2

Spot 3 places that use light to communicate

Lights aren't just for decoration — they're how things talk to us. Have a look around (your room, the street, your phone, a car) and find 3 examples where a light means something specific.

Easy mode: brake lights = "I'm stopping". Phone notification light = "you've got a message". Microwave light = "I'm working".

💡 You'll share one of these in the check-in form at the end.
3

Last class some of you nailed the parallel parking — others didn't quite get there (no stress, we'll smash it this time). Next class the challenge is reverse parking into a garage, plus turning on a reverse light while it backs up.
