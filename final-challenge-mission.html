01 Quick Recap · Everything so far 3 min
Six lessons, four skills. Do you remember how they all fit together?
🎨

RGB & Light Effects

Red, Green, Blue 0–255 mixed together. Breathing lamps fade in and out; gradients shift smoothly from one colour to another.

🛣️

Line Following

mBot2's bottom sensors detect the line and steer to stay on it — the same logic from our sensor lessons.

🖥️

Screen Display

Label Display pins text to a position and overwrites in place — perfect for a clean message on Little M's screen.

🎵

Sound & Music

C D E F G A B = notes 60–71. Beat controls duration. String them together and you've got a song.

Stage 1

Line following works end to end

mBot2 follows the taped line from start to finish without losing it, drifting off, or getting stuck.

Checklist before you call this done:

  • /Robot starts on the line and follows it the whole way without manual correction.
  • /Movement is smooth, not jerky or zig-zagging hard.
  • /It reaches the end of the line and is ready to begin parking.

Stage 2

Parallel parking lands inside the markers

At the end of the line, mBot2 reverses and parks fully between two markers — no crashing, no overshoot.

Set up two markers (boxes, books, anything) with a gap roughly 1.5× the width of mBot2. Checklist:

  • /Robot reverses in a controlled way — not a blind straight backup.
  • /Final position is fully inside the two markers, not touching either one.
  • /It comes to a clean stop — doesn't keep rolling or bouncing off a marker.
Think about itHow will the robot know it's reversed far enough? What sensor or timing trick from earlier lessons could help here?

Stage 3

Light show runs after parking

Once parked, mBot2 plays a short LED sequence using at least 2 colours and 1 effect from earlier lessons.

Pick at least one effect type — mix and match if you want to go further:

  • /Breathing — brightness variable counts 0 → 255 → 0 on a chosen colour.
  • /Gradient — one colour value rises while another falls (e.g. Red → Yellow → Green).
  • /Flashing — alternates between 2+ colours on a timed loop.
ReminderR255 G255 B0 = Yellow · R0 G255 B255 = Cyan · R255 G0 B255 = Magenta. Mix your own from the full RGB table if you want something different.
Scored out of 5
★★★★★
Stage 4

Music finale on Little M

Switch to Little M: show a short message on screen, then play your favourite song — at least 8 notes.

Checklist for the finale:

  • /Screen shows something readable — your group name, "The End!", or similar — using Label Display.
  • /Song uses 8 or more notes from the piano block (C=60 … B=71, any octave).
  • /Tune is recognisable — even a simple melody like Do Re Mi or a song's opening line counts.
  • /Bonus: add a matching LED colour or screen animation while the music plays.
TipHum your song slowly and try to match each note to Do/Re/Mi/Fa/Sol/La/Ti — that's the fastest way to find the right note numbers.
